Il Segreto

The word secret comes from the Latin secernere, “to set aside, to keep separate.” The term, used archaically for agricultural activity, suggests the idea of a selection, a process of inclusion/exclusion, that activates different spaces in which elements can regenerate.

In common usage, secret is a narrative practice that organizes affects and establishes relationships between intimacies. it is a whispered communication that creates minimal, fragile spaces where listening and presence can be negotiated.

The secret becomes the engine of the dance. A group of people whisper secrets to each other and the narrative invents a space of movement, where bodies simultaneously organize themselves, listen to each other, move through voice, constantly modify the image they compose and the environment they cohabit. The discourses that activate the space are barely graspable or inaudible by the spectator: the audience remains simultaneously included/excluded in this game.

This practice - which has to do with that oral transmission of knowledge, often used by women (the healers, the suffragettes) and subjectivities who were barred from a written, public and political outlet - pays homage to all the people who through the exchange of secret information have made and continue to make a difference in their communities and in human history.
